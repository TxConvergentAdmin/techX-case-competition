Mission

Texas TechX Case Competition is a case competition hosted by TC, Texas CYC, and Texas Convergent where teams of 4 students have one week to come up with a solution to a real-life tech based business case before presenting to a panel of judges on Saturday, November 5th. There will be 2 rounds of judging: (1) preliminary case study and (2) grand finale. All undergraduate students from across the university are eligible to participate and can form teams across majors. Students are encouraged to challenge themselves and demonstrate their technical and business skills in collaborative efforts.

FAQ

How should I attend?

Sign up here.

What can I win?

A variety of prizes can be won including cash prizes of $5,000.

How many people per team?

There can be a maximum of 4 people per team and a maximum of 2 people from the same organization.

How much does this cost to attend?

There is no cost but we will be taking $40 per team and returned after the competition to ensure selected teams will compete

What will I be doing?

You will get to explore and manipulate interesting data sets while working with a team and presenting to other teams and judges! You will also get to listen to workshops from our sponsors! Hint: The current dataset has a musical feel.

TC

Student-led strategy consulting group at The University of Texas at Austin that offers pro-bono solutions for Fortune 500s, global NGOs, and startups. Our students come from a broad range of disciplines ranging from finance to biomedical engineering, and demonstrate a commitment to consistent excellence and growth. This enables our teams to consider client projects with a holistic perspective, devise innovative strategies, and pitch actionable business solutions that help maximize our clients’ growth potentials.

Texas CYC

Student-led consulting group, focused on providing pro-bono services to Austin’s small, minority-owned businesses and nonprofits. The organization has worked with clients from all different backgrounds & identities, from a stay-at-home mom to an immigrant family. CYC believes that helping these underserved groups and their businesses thrives makes for vibrant communities and strong economic vitality. For us, consulting is more than a career path or a means for professional development. Consulting is the way we give back to our communities, bridge economic gaps, and truly transform the lives of small business owners.
